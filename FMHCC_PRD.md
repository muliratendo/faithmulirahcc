## 1. Executive Summary

This document outlines the requirements for developing an informational website for Faith Mulira Health Care Centre, a registered Level III health facility located in Masooli, Gayaza Road, near Kampala, Uganda. The website will serve as the primary digital presence for the centre, providing essential information to current and prospective patients about services, location, contact details, and the centre's mission to provide affordable, quality healthcare to the local community.

---

## 2. Product Overview

### 2.1 Purpose
To create a professional, user-friendly informational website that enables patients and community members to easily access information about Faith Mulira Health Care Centre's services, location, operating hours, and contact information, while reinforcing the centre's commitment to affordable, accessible healthcare.

### 2.2 Goals
- Establish a credible online presence for the health care centre
- Communicate the centre's mission of affordable, quality healthcare
- Provide easy access to information about services, especially for the Gayaza/Masooli community
- Showcase the centre as a registered, licensed Level III facility
- Highlight community health initiatives and preventative care programs
- Reduce routine inquiries by providing comprehensive information online
- Build trust within the local community and with prospective patients

### 2.3 Target Audience
- **Primary:** Residents of Masooli, Gayaza, and surrounding areas near Kampala
- Current patients seeking service information
- Expectant mothers looking for maternity services
- Parents seeking vaccination services for children
- Community members needing affordable outpatient care
- Individuals seeking reproductive health services
- Healthcare workers and community health volunteers
- Potential partners and donors interested in community health initiatives

## 4. Non-Functional Requirements

### 4.1 Performance
- Page load time under 5 seconds on 3G mobile connection (accounting for Uganda's connectivity)
- Optimized images (WebP format with fallbacks)
- Minimal JavaScript for faster loading on slower networks
- Compressed assets and efficient code

### 4.2 Compatibility
- **Mobile-First Design:** Priority for mobile devices (majority of users in Uganda access via mobile)
- Responsive design working on feature phones, smartphones, tablets, and desktops
- Cross-browser compatibility (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Opera Mini, UC Browser)
- Support for devices with varying screen sizes (240px to 1920px+)
- Works on low-bandwidth connections

### 4.3 Accessibility
- Simple, clear language (English with potential for Luganda in future phases)
- Large, readable fonts (minimum 16px base)
- High contrast for readability in various lighting conditions
- Touch-friendly buttons and navigation (minimum 44px tap targets)
- Screen reader compatibility
- Keyboard navigation support
- Alternative text for all images

### 4.4 Security
- SSL certificate (HTTPS) for secure browsing
- Spam protection on contact forms
- Basic DDoS protection
- Regular security updates
- Privacy policy compliant with Uganda's data protection requirements
- Secure email handling

### 4.5 SEO & Local Visibility
- **Local SEO Optimization:**
  - Google My Business integration
  - Location-specific keywords (Masooli, Gayaza Road, Kampala)
  - Schema markup for healthcare facility
- Meta tags and descriptions
- Alt text for images
- Sitemap.xml
- Mobile-friendly optimization (Google's mobile-first indexing)
- Fast loading speeds for better rankings

### 4.6 Maintenance
- WordPress or similar CMS for easy content updates
- Training for staff to update basic content
- Regular backup system (daily automated backups)
- Clear documentation in English
- Technical support contact for troubleshooting

## 10. Risks & Mitigation Strategies

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation Strategy |
|------|--------|------------|---------------------|
| Limited internet penetration in target area | Medium | Medium | Focus on mobile optimization, lightweight design, offline-accessible contact info (phone numbers) |
| Low digital literacy among some community members | Low | High | Ensure phone contact remains primary; simple, intuitive design; community education about website |
| Power outages affecting access | Low | Medium | Choose hosting with backup power; optimize for quick loading when connection available |
| Limited budget for ongoing maintenance | Medium | Medium | Choose user-friendly CMS; train internal staff; plan for minimal monthly costs |
| Photography and content gathering delays | Medium | High | Set clear deadlines; prepare alternative stock images; phase content rollout if needed |
| Competing health facilities online | Low | Low | Focus on unique value proposition (affordability, community focus, Level III status) |
| Mobile data costs for users | Medium | Medium | Minimize page sizes; optimize images; provide essential info early on pages |
| Language barriers (English vs. Luganda) | Low | Medium | Use simple English; plan Phase 2 for Luganda; include visual aids and icons |
| Inaccurate location information | High | Low | Verify GPS coordinates; test directions; include multiple landmark references |
| Spam or malicious form submissions | Low | Medium | Implement CAPTCHA or honeypot; email filtering |

## 4. Market Opportunity Analysis

### 4.1 Digital Presence Gap
**Finding:** Level III health centres have minimal to no digital presence, creating significant opportunity for Faith Mulira to establish category leadership.

**Opportunity:**
- First-mover advantage in Level III category for Gayaza/Masooli area
- Capture searches for "health centre near me" in target location
- Build digital credibility among underserved digital audience
- Position as "most accessible" health centre (both physically and digitally)

### 4.2 Mobile-First Opportunity
**Finding:** 85%+ of target audience accesses internet via mobile, but most health facility websites are desktop-oriented.

**Opportunity:**
- Superior mobile experience as key differentiator
- Quick-loading pages for limited data users
- Prominent click-to-call and directions features
- WhatsApp integration for familiar communication channel
- Voice search optimization for local queries

### 4.3 Community Health Positioning
**Finding:** Premium facilities focus on advanced treatments; basic facilities lack community engagement messaging.

**Opportunity:**
- Own the "community healthcare champion" position
- Highlight preventative care and education
- Showcase outreach programs and local impact
- Build emotional connection with local residents
- Differentiate from impersonal premium facilities and basic government centres

### 4.4 Affordability Transparency
**Finding:** Most facilities avoid pricing discussion or emphasize premium services.

**Opportunity:**
- Clear, upfront messaging about affordable care
- Transparent payment options (cash, mobile money)
- Remove financial barriers to seeking information
- Build trust through pricing honesty
- Attract price-sensitive market segment

### 4.5 Local SEO White Space
**Finding:** Weak optimization for neighborhood-specific searches (Gayaza, Masooli, Namugongo Road area).

**Opportunity:**
- Dominate local search results for target area
- Optimize for vernacular location terms
- Capture "near me" searches
- Build citations in local directories
- Leverage Google My Business comprehensively

- Professional photography showcasing real facility and staff
- Clear service categorization with detailed descriptions
- Staff profiles highlighting qualifications
